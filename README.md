# Urban Friction Index: A Citizen-Centric Smart City Analytics Framework

## Overview

Rapid urbanization has increased challenges in city management, including traffic congestion, air pollution, deteriorating road conditions, and inefficient public transportation systems. While modern smart cities generate large volumes of data related to these urban services, most existing systems analyze these datasets independently, limiting the ability of city authorities to understand their combined impact on citizens.

This project proposes a citizen-centric framework that integrates multiple urban indicators into a single metric called the **Urban Friction Index (UFI)**. The framework evaluates how different urban factors collectively influence daily life and identifies areas experiencing the highest levels of inconvenience.

## Objectives

* Integrate traffic, air quality, transportation, and road condition datasets.
* Develop a unified Urban Friction Index (UFI) to measure overall urban inconvenience.
* Identify high-friction urban zones within a city.
* Analyze the contribution of individual factors to urban stress.
* Support city administrators in prioritizing interventions and resource allocation.

## Key Features

* Multi-source urban data integration.
* Urban Friction Index calculation.
* Zone-wise friction analysis and ranking.
* Visualization dashboards and heatmaps.
* Factor contribution analysis.
* Decision-support insights for smart city planning.

## Problem Statement

Current smart city systems evaluate traffic congestion, air quality, transportation performance, and infrastructure conditions separately. This fragmented approach makes it difficult to assess the cumulative impact of these factors on citizens. As a result, city authorities face challenges in identifying critical urban stress zones and making informed planning decisions.

This project addresses the problem by providing a unified framework that quantifies overall urban inconvenience and highlights the major contributors to urban friction.

## Expected Outcomes

* A comprehensive Urban Friction Index for each city zone.
* Identification of urban hotspots with the highest inconvenience levels.
* Actionable insights for urban planners and policymakers.
* Improved resource allocation and smarter city management.
